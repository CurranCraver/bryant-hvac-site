Replacing your heating and air system is one of the larger checks you'll write as a homeowner, and most people only do it once or twice in the years they own a house. So we treat it that way — we size it to your home, lay out the options in plain numbers, and make sure you understand what you're buying before you commit to anything.

Indian Trail sits in the fastest-growing corridor in Union County, and a lot of the homes that went up along US-74 during the boom — out toward Callonwood and Moore Farm — are now hitting the age where the builder-grade equipment is on its way out. If this is your first time replacing a system, you're exactly who we slow down for. The outfit that charges to show up and pushes the biggest, most expensive unit on the lot isn't doing you any favors; an oversized system blasts cold, shuts off fast, and never runs long enough to pull the humidity, so the house feels clammy and the equipment wears out early.

It starts with a load calculation, not a guess. The right size, matched to your square footage and ductwork, is what makes a system quiet, efficient, and long-lived.

## What's Included in Every Install

- A proper load calc and an honest equipment recommendation that fits your budget
- Full removal and haul-away of the old system
- New refrigerant lines, electrical connections, and thermostat setup
- System commissioning — we verify the charge and airflow before we leave
- A walkthrough so you actually know how to run it
- A 2-year labor warranty, backed by the manufacturer's parts warranty, typically 10 years on major components when registered
