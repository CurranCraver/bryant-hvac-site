Almost every emergency call we run in Indian Trail started as something small and cheap that nobody caught in time — a weakening capacitor, a coil that lost half its airflow to dirt, a slow refrigerant leak. A tune-up is how you find those while they're a quiet adjustment instead of a no-cool call at 2am in August, with a houseful of kids and the splashpad at Crooked Creek Park already closed for the night.

Here's the part the big companies don't lead with: a tune-up isn't a sales call. We're not there to invent a reason to sell you a part. The company that charges a fee just to show up has every incentive to find one — we'd rather keep your system healthy so you call us by choice, not by emergency.

The Carolina rhythm is simple: get the AC checked in spring before the first real heat, and the furnace in fall before you need it. Two visits a year keeps a system running closer to the efficiency it had when it was new, which shows up on your power bill as much as in fewer breakdowns.

## What a Visit Actually Covers

- Wash the evaporator and condenser coils so the system can move the heat it's meant to
- Check refrigerant level and look for the early signs of a leak
- Test capacitors, contactors, and electrical connections under load, where most failures begin
- Service the blower and filter, and calibrate the thermostat
- Hand you a written summary in plain language, with no upsell pressure
