Plenty of Mint Hill families have learned to dread the alternative: a company that charges a fee just to show up, sends a stranger, and turns a small fix into a pitch for a whole new system. We don't run that play. If a flame sensor needs cleaning, that's a flame sensor.

Whether you're on gas or electric, the failures we run most:

- **No ignition** — a worn hot-surface igniter, or a flame sensor coated in residue, so the burner catches for a second and drops out.
- **Short-cycling** — on and off every couple of minutes, usually an overheating limit switch, a dirty sensor, or airflow choked by a clogged filter.
- **Blower runs, no heat** — a dead igniter, a tripped rollout switch, or a gas valve that isn't opening.
- **Nothing at all** — a blown control-board fuse, a failed thermostat, or a door safety switch that didn't seat.

## The One Check We Never Skip

On any gas furnace, we inspect the heat exchanger. A cracked exchanger can leak carbon monoxide into the home — a real concern with kids in the house — and that is not something we ever wave off or tell you to watch. If we find a crack, we'll show you exactly what we're seeing and walk through your options before you decide anything.

A deferred heat repair has a way of becoming a midnight emergency, so we'd rather find the small problem in October than meet you in the cold in January. Common igniters, sensors, and control parts ride on the truck, so most repairs are one visit.
