It's a hard-freeze night in January, the house has slid into the 50s, and the furnace clicks but won't catch. That's the call this company was built to answer — a real person picks up, day or night, and we get heat back as fast as the roads from Indian Trail allow.

In a bigger Waxhaw home, the temptation for some companies is to read the address and pad the quote. We don't. If a flame sensor needs cleaning, that's a flame sensor — not a furnace replacement scaled to the square footage.

Whether you're on gas or electric, the failures we run most:

- **No ignition** — a worn hot-surface igniter, or a flame sensor coated in residue, so the burner lights for a second and drops out.
- **Short-cycling** — on and off every few minutes, usually an overheating limit switch, a dirty sensor, or airflow choked by a clogged filter.
- **Blower runs, no heat** — a dead igniter, a tripped rollout switch, or a gas valve that isn't opening.
- **Dead at the thermostat** — a blown control-board fuse, a failed thermostat, or a door safety switch that didn't seat.
