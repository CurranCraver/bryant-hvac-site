You've probably met the alternative: a company that charges a fee just to roll a truck out, sends a tech you've never met, and somehow always lands on a brand-new system. We work the other way. If a $30 capacitor gets you cooling again, that's what you'll hear from us.

The AC calls we run most around Stallings:

- **Air's blowing warm** — usually a failed run capacitor or a contactor that won't pull in, sometimes just a tripped breaker at the condenser.
- **Cooling, but barely** — low refrigerant from a leak, a condenser coil clogged with grass and pollen, or a dirty evaporator choking airflow.
- **Ice on the lines** — almost always low airflow or low charge; we find the cause instead of just thawing it.
- **Won't start at all** — a bad control board, a thermostat wiring fault, or a blown low-voltage fuse.

We stock the parts that fail most — capacitors, contactors, fan motors, refrigerant — on the truck, so the typical repair wraps on the first visit.
