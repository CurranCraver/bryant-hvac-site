Plenty of homeowners have learned to dread the alternative: a company that charges a fee just to come out, sends a stranger, and turns a small fix into a sales pitch for a whole new system. We don't run that play. If a flame sensor needs cleaning, that's a flame sensor, not a furnace replacement.

Whether you're on gas or electric, the failures we run most in Indian Trail are these:

- **No ignition** — a worn hot-surface igniter, or a flame sensor coated in residue, so the burner catches for a second and drops right back out.
- **Short-cycling** — on and off every couple of minutes, usually an overheating limit switch, a dirty sensor, or airflow choked down by a clogged filter.
- **Blower runs, no heat** — a dead igniter, a tripped rollout switch, or a gas valve that isn't opening.
- **Nothing at all** — a blown control-board fuse, a failed thermostat, or a door safety switch that didn't seat.
