Almost every emergency call we run started as something small and cheap that nobody caught in time — a weak capacitor, a coil that lost half its airflow to dirt, a slow refrigerant leak. A tune-up is how you find those while they're a quiet adjustment instead of a 2am no-cool call in the middle of August.

The Carolina pattern is simple: get the AC checked in spring before the first real heat, and the furnace in fall before you need it. Two visits a year keeps a system running closer to the efficiency it had when it was new, which shows up on your power bill as much as in fewer breakdowns.

## What a Visit Actually Covers

- Wash the evaporator and condenser coils so the system can move the heat it's supposed to
- Check refrigerant level and look for the early signs of a leak
- Test capacitors, contactors, and electrical connections under load — where most failures begin
- Service the blower and filter, and calibrate the thermostat
- Hand you a written summary of what we found, in plain language and with no upsell pressure

If you'd rather not track the dates yourself, we offer maintenance agreements that cover both seasonal visits and put you at the front of the line for scheduling when you do need us. That's what the agreement includes — two visits and priority booking, nothing buried in fine print.

We maintain every make and model for homeowners across Monroe and the surrounding county. Our shop is about 15 minutes from Monroe, just off the Highway 74 corridor in Indian Trail, so it's easy to get on the schedule before each season turns.
