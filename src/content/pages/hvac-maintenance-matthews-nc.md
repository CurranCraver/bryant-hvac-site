The older a system gets, the more a tune-up earns its keep — and Matthews has plenty of older systems. Almost every emergency call we run started as something small and cheap nobody caught in time: a weakening capacitor, a coil that lost half its airflow to dirt, a slow refrigerant leak. On a system that's already a couple of decades in, a twice-a-year check is the difference between a quiet adjustment and a no-cool call at 2am in August, with the farmers market at Stumptown Park hours away from opening.

Here's the part the big outfits don't lead with: a tune-up isn't a sales call. We're not there to invent a reason to sell you a part. The company that charges a fee just to show up has every incentive to find one — we'd rather keep your system healthy so you call us by choice, not in an emergency.

The Carolina rhythm is simple: get the AC checked in spring before the first real heat, and the furnace in fall before you need it. Two visits a year keeps a system running closer to the efficiency it had when it was new — which matters most on an aging unit fighting to keep up.

## What a Visit Actually Covers

- Wash the evaporator and condenser coils so the system can move the heat it's meant to
- Check refrigerant level and look for the early signs of a leak
- Test capacitors, contactors, and electrical connections under load, where most failures begin
- Service the blower and filter, and calibrate the thermostat
- Hand you a written summary in plain language, with no upsell pressure

If you'd rather not track the dates yourself, we offer maintenance agreements that cover both seasonal visits and move you to the front of the line when you do need us. That's what the agreement includes — two visits and priority scheduling, nothing buried in fine print.
