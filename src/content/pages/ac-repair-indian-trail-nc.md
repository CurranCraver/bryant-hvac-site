Most "my AC stopped cooling" calls in Indian Trail trace back to a short list, and the symptom points us to the part:

- **Air's blowing but it's warm** — usually a failed run capacitor or a contactor that won't pull in, sometimes just a breaker tripped at the condenser.
- **Cooling, but barely** — low refrigerant from a leak, a condenser coil packed with cottonwood and grass clippings, or a dirty evaporator strangling airflow.
- **Ice on the copper lines** — almost always low airflow or low charge. We find what caused the freeze instead of just thawing it and leaving.
- **Won't start at all** — a bad control board, a thermostat wiring fault, or a blown low-voltage fuse.

We keep the parts that fail most — capacitors, contactors, fan motors, refrigerant — stocked on the truck, so the typical repair gets finished on the first visit instead of a "we'll order it and swing back next week" runaround.
