## Common Furnace Problems We Repair in Monroe

Furnace failures almost always trace back to one of a handful of components. A cracked or failing heat exchanger is the most serious — it can allow carbon monoxide to enter your living space and requires immediate attention. A failed igniter is more common and less urgent, but it means no heat until it's replaced. Blower motor failures prevent your system from moving warm air through the house even when the burner is working correctly. Thermostat and control board issues can mimic more serious problems, which is why accurate diagnosis matters before any parts are ordered.

We carry common replacement parts on the truck so most Monroe furnace repairs are completed in a single visit. If a part needs to be ordered, we'll tell you the timeline honestly and arrange a temporary solution if the situation warrants it.

## Warning Signs Your Furnace Needs Service

Your furnace will usually give you warning before it fails completely. No heat or weak heat from the vents is the most obvious sign, but several subtler signals are worth acting on before they become emergencies. A yellow or flickering burner flame — rather than a steady blue flame — can indicate a combustion problem. Strange noises like banging, rattling, or a high-pitched squeal during operation point to mechanical wear. A carbon monoxide detector alarm anywhere in your home is an emergency: shut the system off and call immediately. A furnace that short-cycles — running briefly and then shutting off before the house reaches temperature — often indicates an overheating issue or a failing limit switch.

Any of these symptoms is worth a diagnostic call before you're sitting in a cold house waiting on a repair that could have been handled during business hours.
