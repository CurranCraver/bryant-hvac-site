It's 2am in January, the house is dropping into the 50s, and the furnace won't light. That's the call we built this company to answer — a real person picks up, day or night, and we get heat back in your home as fast as the roads allow.

Whether you run a gas or electric furnace, the failures we see most often are these:

- **No ignition** — a worn hot-surface igniter or a flame sensor coated in residue, so the burner lights for a second and shuts right back down.
- **Short-cycling** — the furnace turns on and off every couple minutes, usually an overheating limit switch, a dirty flame sensor, or restricted airflow.
- **Blower runs, no heat** — a failed igniter, a tripped rollout switch, or a gas valve that isn't opening.
- **No power at all** — a blown control-board fuse, a bad thermostat, or a door safety switch that didn't seat.

## The One We Take Seriously Every Time

On any gas furnace, we check the heat exchanger. A cracked exchanger can leak carbon monoxide into your home, and that's not something we ever wave off or "keep an eye on." If we find one, we'll show you exactly what we're seeing and walk you through your options before you decide anything.

After more than a decade of these calls across the county, we've seen what deferred repairs turn into. We'd rather find the small problem in October than meet you at midnight in January. We carry common igniters, sensors, and control parts on the truck, so most repairs are a single visit.

We work on gas furnaces, electric furnaces, and heat-pump backup heat, every make and model, for homes across Monroe and the surrounding county.
