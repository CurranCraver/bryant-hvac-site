It's the hottest stretch of July, the upstairs of your home near Cureton is sitting at 80 while the downstairs feels fine, and you're wondering whether the whole system just gave out. In a Waxhaw home, that split is usually a clue, not a catastrophe — and we'd rather find the small cause than sell you a big fix.

That matters here, because a big home tends to draw a big quote. Some companies look at the square footage downtown and assume the budget is there, charge a fee just to show up, and steer you toward a full replacement before they've found anything. We work the other way: if a $30 capacitor or a stuck zone damper is the problem, that's what you'll hear from us.

The AC calls we run most across Waxhaw:

- **Blowing warm** — usually a failed run capacitor or a contactor that won't pull in, sometimes just a breaker tripped at the condenser.
- **Cooling, but weakly** — low refrigerant from a leak, a condenser coil packed with pollen and grass, or a dirty evaporator strangling airflow.
- **One zone won't cool** — on the multi-zone systems common in larger homes, a stuck damper or a failed zone board, not the compressor.
- **Iced-over lines** — almost always low airflow or low charge; we find the cause instead of just thawing it.

We stock the parts that fail most — capacitors, contactors, fan motors, refrigerant — on the truck, so the typical repair finishes on the first visit.
