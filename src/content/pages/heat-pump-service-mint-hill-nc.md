A heat pump is a common setup across Mint Hill because it carries both heating and cooling efficiently through our mild Piedmont winters, pulling warmth from the outside air instead of burning fuel. When one acts up, though, it doesn't fail like a straight AC or a furnace, and that's where the diagnosis earns its keep — especially in a busy household that needs it back online today, not next week.

Here's the promise up front: we don't profit by scaring you. A company that charges a fee just to show up has every reason to find a reason you need a whole new unit. We'd rather fix the reversing valve and have you call us again in five years.

The heat-pump calls we see most:

- **Heats but won't cool, or the reverse** — a stuck or failed reversing valve, the part that reverses refrigerant flow between modes.
- **Outdoor unit frozen solid** — a defrost-cycle fault: a bad defrost board, a failed sensor, or a reversing valve leaving the coil iced.
- **Runs nonstop, never satisfies** — low refrigerant from a leak, which drops capacity in both modes, or auxiliary heat that isn't engaging on schedule.
- **Strip heat running constantly** — usually a charge or control fault forcing the expensive backup heat to carry the load, and you feel it on the power bill.
