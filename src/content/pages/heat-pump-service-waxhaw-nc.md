A heat pump is a common choice in Waxhaw because it carries both heating and cooling efficiently through our mild Piedmont winters, pulling warmth from the outside air instead of burning fuel. When one acts up, though, it doesn't fail like a straight AC or a furnace, and on the variable-speed and multi-zone systems you find in the bigger homes here, the diagnosis is where the job is won or lost.

Here's the promise up front: we don't make money by scaring you. A company that charges just to show up at a large home has every reason to find a reason you need a whole new system. We'd rather fix the reversing valve and have you call us again in five years.

The heat-pump calls we see most:

- **Heats but won't cool, or the reverse** — a stuck or failed reversing valve, the component that reverses refrigerant flow between modes.
- **Outdoor unit frozen solid** — a defrost-cycle fault: a bad defrost board, a failed sensor, or a reversing valve leaving the coil iced.
- **Runs nonstop, never satisfies** — low refrigerant from a leak, which drops capacity in both modes, or auxiliary heat that isn't engaging on schedule.
- **Strip heat running constantly** — usually a charge or control fault forcing the expensive backup heat to carry the whole load, and you feel it on the power bill.

## Repair It, or Plan a Replacement?

Heat pumps generally give you 12 to 15 years.
