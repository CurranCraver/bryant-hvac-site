## When Does Repair Stop Making Sense?

Not every failing HVAC system needs to be replaced — but at some point, repair costs stack up against replacement costs and the math shifts. As a rough guide, if your system is more than 12 to 15 years old and is facing a repair that exceeds half the cost of a new unit, replacement is usually the better financial decision. Older systems also run less efficiently, which means higher monthly utility bills even when they're operating correctly.

We'll give you the honest comparison. If your system can be repaired cost-effectively, we'll say so. If replacement makes more sense for your budget and long-term comfort, we'll lay out the numbers clearly and let you decide.
